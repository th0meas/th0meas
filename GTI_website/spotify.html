An introduction to my music taste

This year's Spotify Wraped was very satisfying for me. My two top artists, Masayoshi Takanaka and Casiopea make music that's in the "Jazz Fusion" genre, something I discovered at the start of the year. Their music is very pleasing and can be heard at every ocation, making it one of the best genres of music out there, in my opinion. Following, Linkin Park, is a very famous band. Their distinctive sound is known all across the world, being one of the bands that pioneered the Nu-Metal sounds, with Mike Shinoda's rap vocals and Chester Bennington's powerfull metal growl and melodic voice, backed up by a very metal sounding band. Their music is filled with emotions, some being relatable in my daily life. Coping with an artist's music and lyrics is something I appreciate when listening to music. Aespa, totally differenet from my top three, is a K-Pop group. In my opinion, the most creative and best sounding group in the k-pop music industry at the moment. They have great visuals and sound, making cool performances in every video clip they release. At number five, Lamp, was my companion at chill moments. Wether I was studying, laying in bed, taking a walk, Lamp is there to chill you out. Their bosa-nova and jazzy sound along with very soft vocals, is the perfect combination to vibe to something that's gonna relax your brain completely.



My music journey started when I was in primary school, using the family laptop to search music on YouTube. I was mainly listening to classic rock songs, but also some of the mainstream pop tracks of the time. I did not give much attention to music back then, but I was taking drumming lessons. In gymnasium, I discovered metal music. It took over my mind completely. I was pretty stuck with metal for a while, but I did listen to other stuff meanwhile as well. Since then, I have discovered many other genres of metal, rock and pop music. As well as hip- hop and rap music. Jazz and the Blues. Tons of Japanese musi... the 'City Pop' genre was my no.1 genre in 2023. I can't think of something else to mention at the moment, but I listen to a lot of music. But, out of them all, I think the band BABYMETAL has won my heart the most. Being in the industry since a young age, the trio basically discovered "cute metal", or "kawaii metal" in Japanese. I am super excited because I am going to see them live at Amsterdam in May 2025. Their music is not for everyone, most of my friends do not understand dhow I can have such a big obsession with them. But, it's definitely my go-to comfort music.
