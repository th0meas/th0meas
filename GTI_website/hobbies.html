Kick Boxing

I started kick boxing in January of 2024, after a friend from work talked about the type of training the do at the gym. I was very interested in starting right away. At first, I rarely visited the gym, mostly due to the exam period at the time, but after March I was really into the sport. That was the period when I started seeing that I was improving, and the training was going by easier for me. Practice there is very demanding, we do lots and different kinds of workouts, including mostly cardio. The sport itself is very interesting. There are many techniques to learn and always improve. The skill ceiling is infinite in kick boxing, and that's what I like most.


Video Games

Video Games have been a part of my life for many years. We had a Wii at our house and I would play with my sisters or friends whenever they came over. I also had a PSP that I would carry around with me in trips and vacations. At 7th grade I got gifted a PS4 and a couple years later I got my first computer, on which I played a lot of games too. Even today, I still game sometimes, trying out new games or revisiting old ones for nostalgic reasons. Also playing with friends is very fun and a nice way to get back with people you havne't talked in a while.

Drums

I started playing the drums when I was 7 years old, and have been playing since. It is a hobby I really love and give a lot of attention to, because I am also a big fan of music in general. My drumming journey started when I wore a t-shirt with a print of Elmo, (a muppet character), playing the drums. I went to my local music school and started lessons with my teacher, who I am friends with, even though I stopped having lessons at 17, two years ago. However, I still play the drums from time to time. It is hard for me to play because I have an acoustic kit and I have placed it in a warehouse at Sindos, close to university.


Bass Guitar

After many years of playing the drums, at 15, I decided to start learning another instrument. It is common for drummers to also play bass, and vise-versa. So, I picked up the bass guitar! I really liked the low frequences that came out of my amplifier - which I had made with my dad in fifth grade, as a fun electronics project! Nowadays, I play the bass more than I do the drums. That's because I have my bass guitar right next to me at home. I started learning the "slap" technique, which is a fun and very funky way to play the bass.
